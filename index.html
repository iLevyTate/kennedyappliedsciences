Active Research

ItaSoRL is an open, reproducible artificial-life study asking when a from-scratch agent comes to represent something it was never asked to care about.

A digital organism is raised inside a simulated world that is sometimes authentic and sometimes a subtly flawed copy. The difference is never rewarded and never named. We read the organism's internal state and ask when it gets encoded anyway.

The gap between what is detectable from the outside and what a mind spontaneously encodes is the object of study. Every experiment is deterministic given its seeds and open to independent replication.

Readout, not reward

A tabula-rasa organism, a surrogate-world ladder, and a linear readout of what it learns without being told to.

Quantitative Algorithmic Trading System

RSSE

A fully automated, rules-based trading engine for TradingView covering futures, stocks, and crypto, built to take emotion out of the trade. Every setup is qualified through a multi-stage signal process, sized risk-first, and managed to its exit, with prop-firm protections and hands-free webhook execution. Across a roughly 6-year backtest, $2,000 grew to a simulated +7,580% return, about 4× buy-and-hold.

Results are simulated and not predictive. RSSE is a research tool, not financial advice.

About the Practice

Kennedy Applied Sciences Research Group is a self-funded applied AI research practice founded by Ben Kennedy, Ph.D.

We explore the boundaries of on-device intelligence, cognitive architecture, decision support, neuromorphic computing, and behavioral technology.

Select tools developed through this research are commercialized as products, validating the underlying science in real-world regulated environments including defense, healthcare, legal, and finance.

The practice is independently operated, takes no outside investment, and exists to protect, publish, and productize its research.
